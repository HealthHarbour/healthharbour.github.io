The Signs of A Concussion

 
  What is A Concussion?

In a nutshell, a concussion is a type of head injury that occurs when the brain is suddenly jolted, bumped, or hit with a blow. Generally, it disrupts the normal functioning of the brain for a relatively short period of time, without necessarily causing visible neurological damage as shown on imaging scans. The first step in recognising the signs of a concussion is to understand what it is.

Violent movements and physical trauma can temporarily injure the brain and affect normal brain function. This can cause a prolonged prevalence of symptoms such as drowsiness, fatigue, nausea, or even death.

The symptoms of a concussion are usually subtle and are slow to appear. The symptoms can last for days, weeks, or even longer.


   Signs in Plain Sight

  • Headache and dizziness
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Memory lapses (temporary memory loss)


   The Subtle Clues

  • Confusion and slurred speech
  • Sensitivity to lights or noise
  • Problems in balancing the body


   Signs That Surface Later

  • Sleep disturbances
  • Emotional changes
  • Difficulty concentrating
