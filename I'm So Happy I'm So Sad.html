I'm So Happy, I'm So Sad: Emotional Transformations During Puberty


During puberty, a person goes through many physical and emotional changes. Many of these changes are the result of new hormones. Hormones are special chemicals that the body starts to produce more of during puberty. They cause your body to grow and change in a lot of different ways. Hormones can also give you mood swings, sometimes without reason. You may feel very happy and all of a sudden feel angry or sad. One minute you’re ecstatic, the next you’re frustrated. Welcome to the emotional whirlwind of puberty! Finding healthy outlets for these emotions and practising self-care are the key to validating your emotions and practising a healthy lifestyle.


Exploring New Feelings

Attraction, infatuation and romantic feelings often start to pop up like unexpected guests. Your brain starts to process new emotions and social dynamics. It's like the brain is rewiring itself.  However, we need to understand that this is all a part of growing up  and maturing, and understanding  these feelings is a huge step toward emotional intelligence.


Self Discovery

Puberty is beyond just physical changes – it's an opportunity for you to get to know yourself on a deeper level. This journey of self discovery is meant to be embraced. This is a journey where you're not just growing taller but also growing wiser and more resilient.
